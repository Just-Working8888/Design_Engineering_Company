Amendments and additions to the RESOLUTION of the Cabinet. Min. KR dated August 6, 2021 No. 114



This Resolution regulates the processes of issuing documents for design, construction, and other modifications of real estate objects, as well as the assessment of compliance for completed construction projects being put into operation in the Kyrgyz Republic in accordance with the "Single Window" principle. It also defines the rights and obligations of individuals and legal entities in the field of architectural and construction activities in line with the legislation of the Kyrgyz Republic.

According to the author of the following text, there are inconsistencies with other laws and regulations. Below are several examples:

  • • In this Resolution, the concept of technical supervision differs from those applied in investment projects, which may lead to ambiguous application of the legislation. It is necessary to supplement the Resolution with corresponding clauses clarifying that certain requirements do not apply to investment projects, or to align them with the applied concepts and requirements.

  • • The term "City-forming structures" according to this resolution refers to buildings and structures that have historical, architectural, and urban planning value, shaping the urban development in accordance with urban planning documentation. If a construction object falls under this term, it is subject to consideration by the urban planning council based on the package of project materials submitted by the applicant, corresponding to the established requirements and composition of documents.
    The urban planning council has the right to extend the deadline for developing the architectural and Urban Planning Conclusion (AGZ) of 20 days, by an additional minimum of 14 days, and in the case of complex structures, this period may be additionally extended for months. As per the requirements of this Regulation, project materials of complex objects that play an important role in shaping the city and have public significance may be sent for expert analysis and opinion, if necessary.

  • • There are numerous such points in this provision, and therefore, a comprehensive review of the document is necessary.

The author believes that it is necessary to provide a detailed description of the term "city-forming objects". For buildings, this may include information on the number of floors, the area of the plot, profile, and other characteristics. For roads and streets, various types and characteristics should be included. For bridges, length, number of lanes, etc. For production facilities, in addition to basic requirements, expert analysis may also be required.
